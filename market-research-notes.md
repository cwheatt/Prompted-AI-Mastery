## Critical AI Literacy Gaps (Collibra Research)
Source: https://www.collibra.com/blog/underestimating-the-need-for-ai-literacy

### The Problem:
- **62% of leaders** recognize AI literacy skill gap in their organizations
- **Only 25%** have implemented organization-wide AI training programs
- **Huge disparity**: Recognition vs. Action

### EU AI Act Compliance Urgency:
- Article 4 mandates organizations ensure staff have "sufficient knowledge, education and training to govern AI effectively"
- Enforcement starts August 2025
- Applies to US companies doing business in EU

### EU AI Act Definition of AI Literacy:
"Skills, knowledge and understanding that allow providers, deployers and affected persons to make informed deployment of AI systems while gaining awareness about the opportunities, risks and potential harm AI can cause."

### Key Insight: 
**AI literacy isn't just for technical teams** - extends to business leaders, policymakers, and end-users

### Risks of Poor AI Literacy:
1. **Negative brand impact** - AI that doesn't align with mission/values
2. **Reputational risks** - Biased AI models erode trust
3. **Operational failures** - Miscommunication between technical/non-technical teams
4. **Weak oversight** - Poor decisions, regulatory risks, blindly trusting flawed AI

### What Organizations Need (Per Collibra):
1. Build common understanding (shared glossary)
2. Train leadership (foundational AI knowledge)
3. Simplify AI understanding for non-technical stakeholders
4. Ensure AI traceability & build trust
5. Foster collaboration (break silos)
6. Learn by doing (70/20/10 model: 70% hands-on experience)
7. Develop social learning (community-driven)
8. Refresh learning continuously (AI evolving rapidly)

### Additional Market Statistics:
- **77% of employees** confused about using AI at work (Forbes)
- **95% of generative AI pilots** at companies are failing (MIT report)
- **Core issue**: "Learning gap" for both tools and organizations
- **77% of companies** allow AI tools at work, but **only 32%** offer training (BambooHR)
- **72% of employers** say employees adequately trained, but **only 53% of employees** agree

### Critical Gap Identified:
**Only 25% of AI training programs address ethics** - troubling gap in preparing professionals for responsible AI use
